tolel, gerund of tol- "coming, arrival", *eb "before (of time only), see eblaer http://eldamo.sourceforge.net/content/words/word-4233047675.html
*eithbi "mosquito", eitha- "to prick, insult" *pí, from Q pí, "small fly".

Björn Fromén Jun 01, 2015 (23:27)

Actually a more probable meaning of eb- is 'after (of time)', cf. the corresponding Q epe. Pointed out by Patrick Wynne on <https://groups.yahoo.com/neo/groups/lambengolmor/conversations/messages/830>.

Björn Fromén Jun 04, 2015 (01:49)

+Hjalmar Holm As Patrick Wynne convincingly argues, Eblaer can't very well mean 'before summer' when the obvious parallel Ephriw means 'Afterwinter', i.e. '[the month] after [the first period of] winter'.

Sadly there is no unambiguous word for 'before (of time)' in either S or Q. Tolkien was notoriously indecisive on this point.
