* ogod; och; uguig n. knee - Gn. octha, Qe. okka : cf. lebed = Gn. leptha &c. (the tentative *uguig is from a CE dual *ôkû, cf. lhewig)

Paul Strack Apr 12, 2015 (20:35)

Of these, I think I like ogod the best. The form uguig is very unlikely. The u-dual was lost before the diminutive suffix -eg was added, so it only survived in rare cases like lhaw. Compare gwanunig and glamog.

ܤܡܝ ܦܠܕܢܝܘܤ Apr 13, 2015 (11:00)

*Ogod would be my favourite too, if I had to pick one. As for *uguig, the assumed existence of *ugu (a natural anatomic pair like lhaw) does not equal assuming any wider survival of the U-dual. From Etym:368 we also see that Tolkien used the suffix -ig to derive lhewig from lhaw.
