Tamas Ferencz Feb 06, 2015 (09:28)

Possibly orya- with an allative can be used in that sense. Otherwise you can use the am(b)a "upwards" prefix, e.g. *ammen- "go up".

ܤܡܝ ܦܠܕܢܝܘܤ Feb 06, 2015 (12:21)

In PE17:182 there is a root RETE 'climb' & an unglossed word retto (cp. against nouns ending in - (t)to), so maybe a verb *ret- (already suggested by Helge in his Q-Eng Wordlist), old frequentative *retta- /retsa- (cp. BAT/Etym:351, Qe lutta- etc.).

The sense has also been assigned to the root RATH (for Sindarin words only?); see Essekenta Endamarwa § 2.26 (3.14 etc.) : http://www.sindanoorie.net/art/RS_TI_WR.html
